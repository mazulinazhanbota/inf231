Popular places

Issyk Lake

A lake in the Issyk gorge of the Zailiyskiy Alatau, formed about 10 thousand years ago (geological age) as a result of a mountain collapse to the East of Almaty, which has its own sad history (a mudflow that happened in the summer of 1963, which took thousands of lives), which is known throughout the Republic of Kazakhstan .

Turgen Gorge

Turgen Gorge is a picturesque place, which is one of the most popular routes in the vicinity of Almaty. There are many sights here, and undoubtedly there is something to see, on the way you can stop by the ostrich farm, the museum of the Scythian warrior Altyn Adam (be sure to visit the Kieli-Bulak spring) and a trout farm.

Medeo

Medeo was and will remain the most beautiful place in Almaty. In all seasons, the beauty of the skating rink pleases the townspeople and guests of the southern capital. Due to its exceptional natural resources and unique engineering and architectural solutions, this sports complex has gained worldwide fame, has become the pride of Almaty residents.

Charyn Canyon

Charyn canyon is a 154 km long canyon along the Charyn River in Kazakhstan. The canyon is located 195 km east of Almaty, not far from the border with China. It is located on the territory of three districts of the Almaty region:Raiymbek, Enbekshikazakh. The canyon is part of the Charyn National Park, formed on February 23, 2004.

Terrenkour

Terrenkour (from the French terrain - the area and cours - the course of treatment) is a method of sanatorium-resort treatment, which provides for dosed physical activity in the form of walking, climbing mountainous terrain along certain marked routes. Terrenkur is a method of training therapy, develops endurance.

Lake Kaindy

Almaty region is known for its picturesque places and one of them is the enchanting lake of incredible beauty - Kayindy. It belongs to the Kolsai Lakes system and is its brightest representative. The reservoir is surrounded by tall spruce trees, but its name means "teeming with birches",- the trunks of ancient firs, turned by time.
